# Behavioral Economics & Cognitive Biases

Depth reference for `marketing-psychology`. Pulled when the workflow needs the
full reasoning behind a principle, not just the one-line summary in the
decision model.

## Loss aversion

People weigh losses roughly 2× as heavily as equivalent gains (Kahneman &
Tversky, Prospect Theory). Use to frame the cost of *inaction* as larger than
the cost of action.

- ✅ "You're losing $X/month to missed calls" — concrete, quantified loss.
- ❌ "Save money!" — vague gain frame, weak.
- Boundary: do not fabricate the loss number. If unknown, say "estimated" or
  omit the figure and use a qualitative frame.

## Social proof

People copy the behavior of similar others, especially under uncertainty
(Cialdini). Three flavors, in order of strength:

1. **User testimonials** — peers, named, with photo + role if possible.
2. **Expert endorsement** — authority in the relevant domain.
3. **Crowd metrics** — "trusted by 10,000+ SMBs" — weakest, easy to dismiss.

Boundary: fabricated testimonials and inflated metrics destroy trust and are
deceptive. Use real ones only.

## Anchoring

The first number exposed shapes the judgment of subsequent numbers. Use to make
a target price feel reasonable by exposing a higher anchor first.

- Pricing tiers: show `Pro $49` next to `Defender $19` — the $49 anchors $19
  as cheap.
- Boundary: a decoy that exists only to anchor, with no intention to sell, is
  legal but erodes trust if discovered. Use real tiers.

## Paradox of choice

More options increase decision paralysis and reduce satisfaction with the
chosen option (Schwartz). Reduce surface options to 2-3; offer "more" only
behind a progressive disclosure.

- ✅ "Choose a plan: Starter or Pro" + "Need more? See all tiers".
- ❌ A 5-row pricing table with every variant on the landing page.

## Zeigarnik effect

People remember interrupted or incomplete tasks better than completed ones.
Surfaces that show "pending" or "incomplete" pull users back.

- Dashboard: "3 missed leads today — review"
- Onboarding: "Your profile is 60% complete — finish to unlock X"
- Boundary: do not manufacture fake incompleteness to drive engagement — that
  is a dark pattern.

## IKEA effect

People value things they helped create more than things handed to them fully
formed. Let users invest small effort early to increase perceived ownership.

- Onboarding: pre-fill business data, let user confirm + customize one field.
- Boundary: do not make the effort *frustrating* — that flips the effect.

## Reciprocity

People feel compelled to return value they received first (Cialdini). Give
something genuinely useful before asking for anything.

- Free tool, free audit, free first month — concrete value, not "free tips".
- Boundary: a "free" thing that is actually a sales pitch is not reciprocity.

## Fogg Behavior Model (B = MAP)

Behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and Prompt converge simultaneously.
Diagnose why a target behavior is not happening:

- **No motivation?** Strengthen the value frame or social proof.
- **No ability?** Reduce friction — fewer steps, less input, smarter defaults.
- **No prompt?** Add the trigger (CTA, notification, contextual nudge) at the
  moment motivation + ability are both high.

## Commitment & consistency

People act in line with their prior stated commitments (Cialdini). Ask for a
small public/stated commitment before asking for the bigger ask.

- "Yes, I want to stop missing calls" → then "Start your trial".
- Boundary: do not extract commitments under false pretenses.
