# Exit Strategy & Valuation

## Valuation Basics

When selling a bootstrapped company, the price is typically:

**Price = Revenue × Multiple**

### Multiples for Bootstrapped SaaS

| ARR Range | Multiple |
|-----------|----------|
| $0-$1M | 2-4x |
| $1M-$5M | 4-6x |
| $5M+ | 6-10x |

### Factors That Increase Multiple

- High growth rate (50%+ YoY).
- Low churn (< 3% monthly).
- High NRR (> 110%).
- Recurring revenue (not one-time).
- Strong moat (proprietary data, network effects).
- Clean financials and documentation.

## Preparing for Sale

### 6-12 Months Before
- Clean up bookkeeping. Messy books scare buyers.
- Get an accountant to review and standardize financials.
- Document processes and IP.
- Reduce owner dependence (automate or delegate).
- Stabilize growth (buyers fear volatility).

### 3-6 Months Before
- Prepare a data room (financials, metrics, contracts).
- Identify potential buyers (competitors, PE firms, strategic acquirers).
- Engage a broker or advisor (optional but helpful for deals > $1M).

### During Due Diligence
- Be transparent. Hidden issues kill deals.
- Respond quickly to requests.
- Keep running the business (don't get distracted).

## Deal Structure

### All-Cash Deal
- Cleanest. Buyer pays, you walk away.
- Best for: Small deals (< $1M), risk-averse sellers.

### Earnout
- Part of the price is paid over time based on performance.
- Risky for seller. Buyer controls the business post-sale.
- **Avoid if possible.** If unavoidable, negotiate:
  - Short earnout period (6-12 months, not 3 years).
  - Clear, achievable targets.
  - Partial payment upfront.

### Equity Swap
- Buyer pays in their company's stock.
- Tax-deferred in some jurisdictions.
- Risky if buyer's stock is volatile.

## Tax Considerations

- **Capital gains vs. income:** Structure the sale to minimize taxes.
- **Country-specific rules:** Consult a tax advisor.
- **Timing:** Consider selling in a lower-income year.
- **Earnouts:** May be taxed as ordinary income (higher rate).

## Exit Checklist

- [ ] Financials are clean and ready for potential sale.
- [ ] Know the approximate valuation of the business.
- [ ] Documented processes and IP.
- [ ] Reduced owner dependence.
- [ ] Identified potential buyers.
- [ ] Prepared data room.
- [ ] Consulted tax advisor on deal structure.
- [ ] Negotiated earnout terms carefully (if applicable).
