# High-Conversion Image Prompting — Social Media Ads

## The Conversion-First Mindset

Before writing ANY prompt, ask: **"What will make someone stop scrolling and click?"**

High-converting images don't just look good — they trigger a psychological response. Every element in the frame should serve a purpose: grab attention, build desire, or drive action.

### The Stop-Scroll Formula

```
ATTENTION (0.2s) → INTEREST (1-2s) → DESIRE (3-5s) → ACTION (click)
```

| Stage | What Happens | Visual Trigger |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **ATTENTION** | Eye catches the image in the feed | High contrast, bold color pop, unexpected composition |
| **INTEREST** | Brain decides if it's relevant | Product category recognition, ingredient familiarity, aesthetic alignment |
| **DESIRE** | Emotional response — "I want that" | Texture, aspiration, sensory cues (steam, droplets, sparkle) |
| **ACTION** | Click-through decision | Clear product visibility, text overlay space, perceived value |

## 1. Visual Psychology — Triggers That Convert

### 1.1 Color Psychology

| Color | Emotional Trigger | Best For | Terra Application |
|-------|------------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Cream / Vanilla** | Warmth, comfort, natural purity | Trust-building, organic positioning | Vanilla base, cream backgrounds |
| **Mocha / Espresso** | Richness, groundedness, energy | Premium positioning, morning ritual | Coffee scrub, deep earthy tones |
| **Dusty Mauve / Blush** | Femininity, softness, luxury | Self-care, romantic positioning | Swirl accents, bath products |
| **Sage / Eucalyptus** | Calm, cleanliness, spa | Relaxation, wellness | Botanical accents, spa scenes |
| **White / Marble** | Purity, premium, clinical-clean | Luxury positioning, hero shots | Clean backgrounds, text-safe zones |
| **Amber / Gold** | Warmth, value, richness | Gift positioning, premium feel | Accent lighting, candlelit scenes |

**Rule of 3:** Never use more than 3 colors in a single image (including background). The brain processes triads faster than complex palettes.

### 1.3 The "Face-ism" Effect

Images containing faces or hands perform **~35% higher engagement** on social media than product-only shots.

| Technique | How to Apply in AI | Prompt Example |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Hand holding product** | Show hands gripping, holding, or interacting with the product | `hands holding the soap bar, fingers gently wrapped around` |
| **Hand applying product** | Hands in the act of using the product | `hand scooping the body scrub, product in palm` |
| **Hands in frame only** | No face needed — just hands create human connection | `two hands, one holding the soap, the other sprinkling salt crystals` |
| **Reflection/mirror** | Partial face visible through mirror | `reflection in mirror, face partially visible, product in foreground` |

**⚠️ Caution:** AI models struggle with hands (fingers, proportions). Keep hands at the edge of frame or partially obscured. Use Ideogram for better hand rendering.

### 1.3 Sensory Cues That Trigger Desire

| Cue | What It Signals | Prompt Technique |
|-----|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Steam** | Warmth, comfort, active use | `steam rising gently from a warm bath` |
| **Water droplets** | Freshness, moisture, hydration | `fine water droplets glistening on the glass jar` |
| **Sparkle / catchlight** | Quality, value, premium | `light catching the edge of crystalline salt, sparkle` |
| **Texture contrast** | Tactile quality, craftsmanship | `rough burlap next to smooth creamy soap, tactile contrast` |
| **Soft shadows** | Depth, realism, not AI-generated | `soft natural shadows grounding the product` |
| **Imperfections** | Handmade, authentic, artisanal | `tiny air bubbles in the handcrafted soap, proof of handmade process` |

### 1.4 Visual Persuasion Techniques

| Technique | How It Works | Prompt Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Scarcity cues** | Single product, isolated, precious | `one single bar of soap, centered, hero lighting` |
| **Social proof** | Multiple products together (set, collection) | `three scents displayed together, gift set arrangement` |
| **Authority markers** | Clinical/clean presentation for efficacy | `clean white background, precise composition, scientific lighting` |
| **Reciprocity trigger** | Generous ingredients, abundance | `generous heap of salt crystals, overflowing ingredients` |
| **Curiosity gap** | Partial view, hidden details | `soap partially wrapped in linen, one corner visible` |

### 1.5 Subliminal Scanning Patterns

| Pattern | How It Works | Composition Strategy |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Z-Pattern** | Western viewers scan left-to-right, top-to-bottom in a Z shape | Product top-left → ingredient top-right → CTA bottom-right |
| **F-Pattern** | Text-heavy images: two horizontal scans + left vertical | Text on left 40%, visual on right 60% |
| **Eye-line cues** | Gaze follows where a model/subject looks | Model looks at product → viewer looks at product |
| **Hidden directional cues** | Lines, triangles, and shadows subconsciously point | Linen folds radiating toward product, shadow arrows |
| **Rule of thirds** | 3×3 grid intersections = natural attention anchors | Product at upper-left or upper-right intersection |

### 1.6 The "Information Gap" (Curiosity Loop)

Images that show JUST enough to trigger curiosity but not enough to satisfy it:

- **Cropped ingredient** — Frame cuts through a lavender stem or salt pile → brain wants to see "the rest"
- **Partially obscured product** — Product half-hidden behind a prop (towel, hand, leaf)
- **Reflection imagery** — Product reflected in water/mirror showing a different angle
- **"What's next?" carousel** — First image is a closed jar, second is opened/revealed

**Prompt technique:** `[PRODUCT] partially obscured by [PROP] in the foreground, creating visual curiosity and information gap`

## 2. The 3-Concept Framework — Enhanced for Conversion

### Concept 1: Ingredient Story (Primary Ad — Sell the Craft)

**Psychological trigger:** Transparency, trust, artisanal quality. The customer sees the raw ingredients and feels "this is real, this is honest, this is high quality."

**Stop-scroll element:** High contrast between product color and surface. Scattered ingredients create visual texture that stands out in a feed.

**Composition:** Rule of thirds or golden ratio. Product on one side, ingredient trail flowing toward it. Leading lines from ingredients to product.

**Lighting:** Warm natural window light or golden hour. Soft shadows. Feels like a morning kitchen counter.

**Text-safe zone:** Top 25% of frame should be clean background (wall, bokeh, or seamless).

**Prompt template:**
```
Professional product photography of [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION] on a [SURFACE] surface,
surrounded by [INGREDIENT 1], [INGREDIENT 2], and [INGREDIENT 3].
[PRODUCT COLOR AND TEXTURE].
[PROPS].
[LIGHTING].
Shot with [LENS SPECS].
[COLOR PALETTE].
[STYLE KEYWORDS].
Ingredient-focused composition. Generous negative space above for text.
No text, no overlays, no UI elements.
[IDENTITY ANCHOR — if I2I]
```

### Concept 2: Spa/Lifestyle Context (Carousel — Sell the Feeling)

**Psychological trigger:** Aspiration, emotional projection. The customer imagines themselves in the scene — warm bath, candles, steam, self-care.

**Stop-scroll element:** Warm lighting + steam + atmospheric depth. The "feeling" is the hook, not the product.

**Composition:** Centered or slightly off-center. Product in lower third. Atmospheric elements (steam, bokeh, candles) in upper frame.

**Lighting:** Candlelight, practical lighting, or golden hour. Warm temperature (2700-3200K). Deep shadows for mood.

**Text-safe zone:** Upper third (steam/bokeh area) or lower left corner.

**⚠️ Watch out:** Color accuracy can drift in warm lighting. Keep product colors explicit in prompt.

**Prompt template:**
```
Product photography of [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION] in a [SETTING].
[PRODUCT APPEARANCE]. It sits on [SURFACE] beside [PROPS].
[ATMOSPHERE].
[LIGHTING].
Shot with [LENS SPECS].
[COLOR PALETTE].
Tranquil spa aesthetic. Warm luxurious atmosphere.
No text, no overlays.
[IDENTITY ANCHOR — if I2I]
```

### Concept 3: Macro Texture (Carousel End — Sell the Quality)

**Psychological trigger:** Haptic desire — "I want to touch it." This is the detail shot that justifies the price. The texture IS the selling point.

**Stop-scroll element:** Ultra-sharp macro detail. The contrast between sharp focus and creamy bokeh creates depth that stops the eye.

**Composition:** Extreme close-up. One sharp focal plane. Product fills 80%+ of frame. Bokeh background.

**Lighting:** Side grazing light (raking light) at 15-20° from parallel to surface. Reveals every micro-texture.

**Text-safe zone:** Lower third or overlay on bokeh area.

**Prompt template:**
```
Extreme close-up macro product photography of [PRODUCT PART].
Razor-sharp focus on [KEY TEXTURES AND INGREDIENTS],
with [BACKGROUND] in creamy bokeh.
Shot with [MACRO LENS SPECS: 90mm f/2.8 macro].
[LIGHTING].
Rich tactile texture, [COLOR TONES].
[STYLE].
No text, no overlays, no UI elements.
```

## 3. Prompt Engineering — The "Identity Anchor" Pattern

### The Core Principle

I2I prompts must explicitly answer two questions:
1. **WHAT TO KEEP** — Identity-preserving constraints (shape, texture, color, ingredients)
2. **WHAT TO CHANGE** — Scene elements (background, lighting, props, composition)

### Structure

```
[SCENE DESCRIPTION: What to change — background, lighting, props, composition]
+
[IDENTITY ANCHOR: What to keep — shape, texture, color, decorative elements]
```

### Identity Anchor Templates

**For soap bars:**
```
While maintaining the same [rectangular/round] form,
[toppings/details on top], [texture pattern],
and the [color descriptor] earthy color of the original soap.
```

**For jars/bottles:**
```
While keeping the same [jar/bottle] shape,
[label design] placement,
and the [product color] of the original.
```

### Strength Parameters by Concept

| Concept | Nano Banana Pro | Ideogram v4 | Why |
|---------|----------------|-------------|-----|
| Ingredient Story | 0.70 | 0.65 | Scene change significant, keep product |
| Spa/Lifestyle | 0.75 | 0.70 | Big scene change, need higher strength |
| Macro Detail | 0.70 | 0.65 | Zoom + texture focus, lower is safer |
| Studio Hero | 0.80 | 0.75 | Clean background swap, product holds at higher strength |

## 7. Common Failures & Fixes

| Failure | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| **Product color drifts** | Too high strength, no color in prompt | Strength ≤0.75 (Nano) / ≤0.70 (Ideogram). Add explicit color descriptor + identity anchor |
| **Product shape changes** | No shape specification | Add shape to prompt: `rectangular soap bar`. Add identity anchor |
| **Text is gibberish** | Using FLUX/Nano for text | Use Ideogram with `style_type: DESIGN` or add text as post-processing overlay |
| **Cluttered composition** | Too many props, no composition guide | `clean minimalist composition, generous negative space, limit props to 2-3` |
| **Inconsistent campaign lighting** | Different lighting per image | Standardize: Ingredient = window light, Lifestyle = warm ambient, Macro = soft studio |
| **Product looks AI-generated** | Too perfect, no texture descriptors | `handcrafted, visible air bubbles, natural imperfections, hand-cut edges` |
| **Product lost in scene** | Strength too high | Lower strength. See strength table above for exact values |

## 8. Model-Specific Notes

### Nano Banana Pro Edit
- **Strength sweet spot:** 0.65–0.75 for product ads
- **Input:** `image_urls` (array) — always use `[url]` not `url`
- **Best for:** Creative scene interpretation, strong product identity preservation
- **Prompt style:** Prose, 30–120 words. Lead with scene, end with identity anchor

### Ideogram v4 I2I
- **Strength sweet spot:** 0.60–0.70 for product ads
- **Input:** `image_url` (string) — NOT array
- **Best for:** Clean commercial look, structured quality, color accuracy
- **Style_type:** `REALISTIC` for product/ingredient, `AUTO` for lifestyle, `DESIGN` for text
- **Prompt style:** Prose, 50–150 words. Subject → setting → lighting → composition → style
- **Returns:** JSON prompt deconstruction in response (model's understanding of your prompt)
